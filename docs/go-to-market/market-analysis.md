# Market Analysis Framework

*This document demonstrates market analysis methodology. Figures are illustrative examples showing analytical approach, not validated market data.*

## Market Opportunity Assessment

### Regulatory Drivers

**EU AI Act (enforced 2025):**
- Mandatory fairness testing for high-risk AI
- Affects: Credit scoring, underwriting, algorithmic trading, KYC/AML

**US ECOA:**
- Ongoing disparate impact testing requirement
- Affects: Lenders, credit card issuers, mortgage companies

**UK FCA / Singapore MAS:**
- AI governance frameworks
- Affects: Regulated financial institutions

### Addressable Market Segmentation

**Example Segmentation Framework:**

| Segment | Regulatory Urgency | Buying Propensity | Entry Strategy |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| EU Banks | High (2025 mandate) | High | Direct sales, regulatory positioning |
| US Lenders | Medium (ongoing) | Medium | Case studies, integration partnerships |
| FinTechs | Medium (varies) | Medium | API-first, product-led |

### Competitive Landscape Framework

**Direct Competitors:**
- Enterprise AI governance platforms
- Strengths: Comprehensive features, real-time monitoring
- Gaps: High cost, complex implementation, not finance-specific

**Indirect Competitors:**
- Open-source libraries (Fairlearn, AIF360)
- Strengths: Free, transparent, academically validated
- Gaps: Requires coding expertise, no regulatory templates

**DIY Solutions:**
- Custom internal scripts
- Strengths: Tailored to specific needs
- Gaps: Inconsistent, unmaintained, poor documentation

### Differentiation Strategy

**Positioning:**
- **Focus:** Financial services specialization vs. generic AI governance
- **Accessibility:** No-code interface vs. developer libraries
- **Compliance:** Pre-built regulatory templates vs. generic frameworks
- **Pricing:** Efficiency-focused vs. enterprise overhead
